What this resource covers

A focused editorial and coaching perspective, written specifically for women.

This site exists because many of the questions women bring into coaching — about identity, confidence, visibility, worth, and change — deserve their own space. Not a watered-down version of general growth content, but something attentive to the particular ways women navigate transition, authority, and self-expression.

The Curious Bonsai for Women draws on the coaching and therapeutic perspective of The Curious Bonsai practice in Singapore, with a specific lens on women's empowerment, career transition, entrepreneurship, and the quieter but significant work of self-acceptance and voice.

Core themes

What we explore here

Each theme represents a genuine area of coaching work — not a checklist, but a lens through which women's growth becomes visible and navigable.

Confidence & Self-Trust

Confidence is rarely about knowing more or doing more. It usually comes from a deeper relationship with your own judgment — and from recovering the parts of yourself you learned to hide. This theme explores what confidence actually looks like when it's built from the inside out.

Career Transitions

Career change is rarely just a professional decision. For many women, it carries questions about identity, worth, and what it means to start again. These articles explore how to navigate transition with more self-awareness, less panic, and a clearer sense of direction.

Voice & Self-Expression

Many women arrive at a point where they realise they've been speaking in a register that isn't quite theirs — careful, qualified, reduced. Reclaiming your voice is not about being louder. It's about finding the tone that actually belongs to you, and having the confidence to use it.

Women in Business

Female entrepreneurship brings its own particular set of internal challenges — visibility, pricing, authority, and the quiet pressure to justify taking up space. This theme offers coaching-informed perspectives for women building something of their own.

Self-Acceptance

Self-acceptance is often the foundation everything else is built on — and one of the most quietly difficult pieces of personal growth work. Not the Instagram version, but the slow, honest process of being at peace with who you are while still allowing yourself to grow.

Wealth, Worth & Expansion

Wealth is not just a financial conversation. For many women it is also a conversation about permission — permission to want more, charge more, and occupy more space. This theme explores the relationship between self-worth and financial clarity with nuance and practicality.

A perspective from the practice

The work behind this resource

Michelle Mah is a psychotherapist and coach based in Singapore. Her work explores how women grow through change — how they reconnect with self-trust, reclaim their voice, and build lives and careers that feel more aligned, expressive, and grounded.

Over the years, she has worked with women navigating career crossroads, identity shifts, confidence blocks, and the particular challenge of stepping into greater visibility without losing themselves in the process. Much of what appears on this site reflects patterns and perspectives that emerge from that real, ongoing work.

This site is not a personal brand project. It is a resource — a place to make coaching-informed thinking available to women who are in the middle of growing, and who want something more thoughtful than generic advice.

Michelle Mah, M.Couns

Psychotherapist & Coach · The Curious Bonsai

Part of a wider body of work

This site is rooted in The Curious Bonsai

The Curious Bonsai for Women is a topic-focused initiative within the broader work of The Curious Bonsai — a therapy and coaching practice in Singapore committed to thoughtful, evidence-informed personal growth.

The practice was founded by Michelle Mah and Stuart Tan, and brings together psychotherapy, executive coaching, and personal development with a depth of care that distinguishes it from generic coaching services. The work here reflects that same commitment — applied specifically to the questions, transitions, and growth edges that women face.

If you're looking for direct professional support — through therapy, one-on-one coaching, or a more sustained engagement — you're warmly invited to explore the wider practice.
